§ 04 / The Pact

The Operator's Pact.

There are roughly thirty-seven thousand objects in low Earth orbit large enough to be tracked, and the great majority of them are tumbling, irregular, uncooperative, and impossible to grab. Every company in this market has been quietly assuming someone else would solve that — that the targets would magically arrive already stabilized, already characterized, already willing. They will not.

Pre-stabilization is the unsolved dependency. It is the part of the value chain that nobody has demonstrated, because demonstrating it is hard, and because the people with the capital have been looking elsewhere — at autonomy, at AI, at software eating the physical world. The physical world is still there. It still tumbles. It still has mass.

Delta Infinite is a physical-systems company solving a physical-systems problem. We are building the foam interface — the thing that meets a tumbling object on its own terms and makes it tractable for everything that comes after. Capture, repair, deorbit, recycle, refuel, mine. None of it works without this layer. This layer is the unlock.

The plan is unromantic and stubborn. License what we build to the operators near term, generate the heritage and the revenue, and use that runway to become an operator ourselves on the timeline that matters — cislunar, water, depots, the trillion-dollar infrastructure that follows. There is a long walk between here and there. We've been walking it since 2012.

You are part of this now. Not symbolically. The merchandise was the credential, and you wore it before there was a reason to. That mattered. That continues to matter. When a mission flies, you'll have been here.

— Kai Cahlil, Founder Delta Infinite · 2026
